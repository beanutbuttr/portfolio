tl;dr
As a film student, one of my favorite websites is www.screenmusings.com

I use it to look for screen caps of my favorite movies. Screenmusings is usually my go-to, but navigating the site can be tricky sometimes. So, I decided to give the site a makeover.
FINDING PROBLEMS
What exactly didn't I like about the original website?

  • small font-size headings in low contrast colors
  • multiple navigation bars - some lead to the same pages, some lead to different pages
  • Some movies appear twice (like 2001: A Space Odyssey), and each has a different set of images. It seems this is because one link is for DVD captures and the other contains Blu-Ray captures. It would be nice to have a way to distinguish this!
  • Nav bar “filters” on the left do not always reload with clear headings - for example, the “Trailers” filter still leaves the heading as Index of Movies, whereas clicking on other filters does update the header (ie. Index of Blu-ray Movies, Index of Cinematographers, etc).
  • also interesting - on mobile, the left vertical navbar collapses and merges with the top navbar
  • all movies are organized in alphabetical order but the column-wrap order can make ordering ambiguous - I always have to double-check whether it is alphabetical in the column direction or row direction.
  • On mobile, you can click “more” to reveal a dropdown menu, but there is no way to collapse it.
  • some of the entries are music videos, which do not contain director/cinematographer information. I didn’t expect a movie site to include music videos.
  • Inconsistency in director names, ex. Park Chan-wook vs Chan-wook Park
A lot of things to consider - so, I started with some lo-fi prototypes to get a simple design down.
