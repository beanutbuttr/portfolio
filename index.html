VR Piano Visualizer
Inspired by my piano students who watch reactive piano visualizers on YouTube to help them practice at home, I created an application that visualizes midi data in virtual reality.

While 2D visualizers are able to represent note pitch and duration on a piano, they are unable to represent the volume of each note in an intuitive way. In the VR Piano Visualizer, volume is represented by note height.

This application was created using Unity and Fusion 360. Features include:
Wayfinding
Volume represented by height of the key
Choose from 5 songs
Play/Pause playback
Slow down and Reset speed

Descent: A Sea Exploration Game
As a capsule recently descended into a caldera, explore your new surroundings and meet the creatures that lie below!

What does it mean to find mutual understanding with the completely alien? How do you renegotiate space peacefully with the environment you find yourself in, and can you make yourself part of that world without compromising your own identity? What do you become when you fully commit to the understanding of another world? Our game seizes around the idea of not only exploring a space but internalizing space, the way that the spaces we exist in increase our capacity for empathy and understanding if we are willing to listen to them.

Small desktop game created with Unity. Responsibilities included scripting interactions and movement in C#, and consolidating teammates’ models, animations, and sounds into Unity.
Image to LaTex
A character-by-character convolutional neural network model to convert handwritten Math equations/symbols into LaTeX markup language.

Our model takes in an image that contains one or more handwritten equations on separate lines, with the constraint that each equation can be read purely from left to right– this does not include characters that contain a symbol above or below, such as fractions or summation notation.

Responsibilities included translating CNN output into LaTeX markup.

Hey Diary, I think I'm in love: Short Film
My short film called “Hey Diary, I think I’m in love” is about a journal entry and a love for all kinds of things. A girl goes to the park to write in her journal about things she loves — simple things like balloons and trees, but also the things she used to love like jumping on the couch and poking her baby sister’s cheeks.

When you meet someone, one of the first things you share are the things you love - your interests, passions, favorite foods and favorite memories. This short film is dedicated to those moments.

Directed, written, and shot by me.
